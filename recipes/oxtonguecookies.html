Ox Tongue Cookies

Back to recipes

What are Ox Tongue Cookies?

A traditional cookie that’s flavored with molasses and spices, ox tongue cookies are named for their shape. No oxen are involved. The ox tongue cookie’s history dates to the mid-1800s and is rooted in the Lehigh Valley’s Dutch community, says William Woys Weaver, noted expert and author on Pennsylvania Dutch cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 3/4 ts Cinnamon
  • 3/4 ts Cloves
  • 2 1/2 c Flour
  • 1/4 c Hot water
  • 1 tb Vinegar
  • 1/4 c Brown sugar
  • 3/4 ts Nutmeg
  • 1 c Molasses
  • 1/2 c Lard
  • 2 ts Soda; dissolved in

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 350F
  2. Place molasses in mixing bowl, heat lard and pour over molasses.
  3. Add brown sugar, soda in water and vinegar. Stir.
  4. Sift in spices with flour and beat well.
  5. Shape bits of dough in a tongue shape and roll in granulated sugar.
  6. Bake on sheet 350 F. about 15 mins.
